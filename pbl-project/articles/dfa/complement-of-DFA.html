Complement of a DFA: Flipping Accept/Reject States

Easy 8 min read Last updated: March 2024

üéØ What is DFA Complementation?

Think of complementing a DFA like creating an "opposite day" version of your DFA - everything that was accepted is now rejected, and everything that was rejected is now accepted! It's like flipping a switch: YES becomes NO, and NO becomes YES.

How to Complement a DFA

Just Two Simple Steps:

  1. Make accepting states non-accepting

    All states that were accepting (final) become non-accepting

  2. Make non-accepting states accepting

    All states that were non-accepting become accepting (final)

üí° Important: The transitions stay exactly the same!

Quick Summary

  • Complementing a DFA just means flipping all accept/reject states
  • The structure and transitions stay exactly the same
  • It's like creating an "opposite" version of your original DFA
  • Useful for creating "NOT" conditions in pattern matching
