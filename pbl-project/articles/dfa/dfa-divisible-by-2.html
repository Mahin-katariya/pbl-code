DFA for Even Binary Numbers

Easy 10 min read Last updated: March 2024

üéØ Our Goal

We want to build a DFA that can recognize binary numbers that are even. Here's the trick: in binary, a number is even if and only if it ends with 0! For example, 1010 (10 in decimal) is even, while 1011 (11 in decimal) is odd.

Examples

‚úÖ Even Numbers (Accept):

  • 0 (decimal: 0)
  • 10 (decimal: 2)
  • 100 (decimal: 4)
  • 1010 (decimal: 10)

‚ùå Odd Numbers (Reject):

  • 1 (decimal: 1)
  • 11 (decimal: 3)
  • 101 (decimal: 5)
  • 1011 (decimal: 11)

DFA Design

States We Need:

  • q0: Even numbers (accepting state)
  • q1: Odd numbers

Current State Input: 0 Input: 1
‚Üí *q0 (Even) q0 q1

Let's Walk Through an Example

Processing the string "1010" (decimal 10)

  1. Start at q0 (Even state)
  2. Read '1': Move to q1 (Now odd)
  3. Read '0': Move to q0 (Now even)
  4. Read '1': Move to q1 (Now odd)
  5. Read '0': Move to q0 (Now even)
  6. End at q0 (accepting state) ‚úÖ Number is even!

Quick Summary

  • A binary number is even if it ends in 0
  • We only need two states: even (q0) and odd (q1)
  • Reading a 0 keeps even numbers even, and makes odd numbers even
  • Reading a 1 makes even numbers odd, and keeps odd numbers odd
